## Version 2 — Operator voice, technical credibility

Five things every Toowoomba termite inspection should answer at the site visit:

1️⃣ **Chemical-barrier status documented.** Look at the Termite Treatment Certificate under the meter box. Install date, chemistry used, expected lifespan. If there isn't one, the property has been unprotected for some time. Pre-1995 = organochlorine, expired. Post-1995 = permethrin/bifenthrin, 10-15yr life. Modern = Termidor/Premise, 8-10yr life.

2️⃣ **Reticulation system check (1995-2008 builds).** If the slab has reticulation pipework, when was it last re-charged? Most owners don't even know they have it. Easy fix: $1,200-$2,500 to re-charge with Termidor SC if pipes are intact.

3️⃣ **Sub-floor moisture readings.** Anything above 15% in framing is termite-friendly. Above 20% is active risk. Canal-estate and wetland-edge properties (Rangeville, Hope Island, Sanctuary Cove) routinely measure 18-25%.

4️⃣ **Coptotermes nest scan within 100m.** The dominant Toowoomba species nests centrally and forages up to 100m through soil to hit timber. Check tree stumps, landscape timbers, fence-line piers, sub-floor framing of all outbuildings.

5️⃣ **AS 3660.2 written report with photos + moisture log.** Not a one-page "all good" tick sheet. A real report has 8-12 pages with photos of every suspect area, moisture readings logged by location, and treatment recommendations.

Without these five, the inspection isn't AS 3660 compliant — and your insurance won't recognise it as a valid inspection.
