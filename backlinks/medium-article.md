## Body

Buying a home on the Toowoomba in 2026? Here's a number that should focus your attention: roughly **15 to 20 percent** of pre-purchase termite inspections we conduct turn up active termite evidence or significant past damage. Most of which the seller hadn't disclosed.

Buyers walk into Toowoomba settlement assuming the building report covers everything. It doesn't. Termite damage is its own category — usually excluded from home insurance, frequently missed by general building inspectors, and (on the Toowoomba specifically) accelerated by year-round Coptotermes activity in coastal QLD soil.

Here's what every Toowoomba homeowner — buyer or owner — should know.

### Coptotermes acinaciformis is the dominant pest, and it's the worst

Australia has roughly 350 termite species. Three are economically destructive. On the Toowoomba, the dominant species — found in about 80% of active infestations — is **Coptotermes acinaciformis**, the most destructive termite on the continent.

Coptotermes builds large central colonies (often inside a tree trunk or buried wood source within 100 metres of the house), then sends foraging tunnels through soil to hit timber wherever it finds it. A small colony can scale to over a million termites inside five to seven years. They never go dormant — coastal QLD humidity and warm soil temperatures keep them active year-round.

### Why the Toowoomba risk is higher than most of Australia

Three factors stack on top of each other.

**(1) Humidity and soil moisture.** Toowoomba averages 70-85% relative humidity year-round. Sub-floor crawl spaces in older homes stay damp. Damp timber is termite food. Damp soil is termite habitat.

**(2) Coastal building styles.** Pre-1985 Toowoomba residential stock — most of the original beachside grid in Mermaid Beach, Burleigh Heads, Palm Beach, Centenary Heights and Coolangatta — is timber-framed cottage on a sub-floor crawl space. That's the textbook termite-attack target. Slab-on-ground stock from 1990s-2000s switched to chemical reticulation systems for protection — but those systems hit end of life around 8-15 years and many haven't been renewed.

**(3) High natural colony density near reserves and waterways.** Properties within 50m of the Rangeville Lakelands Conservation Area, Centenary Heights Valley bushland, or Tamborine Mountain forest interface face 5-10x the natural termite pressure of inland suburbs. Same for the canal estates — high water table + landscape-rich gardens.

### What AS 3660 actually requires (and most homeowners skip)

The Australian Standard AS 3660.2-2017 expects every property to have a competent annual termite inspection. It's not a black-letter law, but it's the standard the courts use to determine whether "reasonable care" was taken. If your house gets eaten by termites and you have no record of inspections, your insurance won't pay, AND you have no recourse against the previous owner or building inspector.

The methodology AS 3660 requires is specific:

1. Sub-floor crawl space (where accessible). Every joist, bearer, pier inspected. Moisture-meter readings.
2. Roof void. Every truss, top plate, rafter.
3. Perimeter / external. Slab edge, weep holes, brick courses.
4. Garden and outbuildings. Coptotermes nests within 100m.
5. Interior wet areas. Bathrooms, kitchen, laundry.
6. Suspect-timber probing with moisture meter and (where needed) thermal imaging.

A 20-minute walk-around is not an AS 3660 inspection. Demand the full methodology.

### The reticulation system expiry wave

If your home was built between 1995 and 2008, it almost certainly has a **chemical reticulation system** — perforated polyethylene pipes laid in the slab footprint and the perimeter, designed to be replenished with termiticide every 5-10 years.

A 2003-built Highfields home should have had reticulation top-ups in roughly 2010, 2018 and 2025. If the owners (or any previous owners) skipped those, the system is now chemically empty — the pipes are still there but they do nothing.

Roughly 40% of Toowoomba homes from this build era have expired or never-renewed reticulation, the owners blissfully unaware. The fix is easy and cheap: re-charge the existing reticulation with Termidor SC or Premise 200SC — about $1,200 to $2,500. Far cheaper than installing a new system from scratch ($3K-$5K) or dealing with damage after the fact.

### Pre-1995 chemical barriers are fully expired

Properties built before 1995 likely had a chlordane or dieldrin chemical barrier — banned since 1995 but the residue is still in soil. These treatments were exceptional (40+ year life) but the chemistry is degraded now. A 1985-installed organochlorine barrier is no longer providing meaningful protection.

Pre-1995 housing stock includes most of beachside Mermaid Beach, Burleigh Heads, Centenary Heights, Palm Beach, Glenvale behind the towers, and Wilsonton. **Every one of these properties should have either a current chemical barrier renewal or a baiting system in place.** Many don't.

### What every Toowoomba home should have on file

The four documents the next buyer's pre-purchase inspector will ask for:

1. **Current Termite Treatment Certificate** under the meter box, showing chemical / baiting system, install date, expected lifespan.
2. **AS 3660.2 annual inspection reports** for at least the last 3 years.
3. **Receipt history** showing chemical-barrier renewal at expiry, or baiting-system annual monitoring.
4. **For older stock (pre-1995):** proof that the original organochlorine barrier was renewed with a modern non-repellent at appropriate intervals.

No paper trail = no insurance coverage = full liability if termite damage is later found.
